# Upcoding

## Definition

Upcoding is a healthcare fraud practice where a provider bills for a more expensive service than was actually performed.

## Example

Actual Service:
Office Visit (Level 2)

Claim Submitted:
Office Visit (Level 5)

## Why it is Fraud

The provider receives higher reimbursement from Medicare.

## Fraud Indicators

- High frequency of expensive procedure codes
- Mismatch between diagnosis and billed procedure
- Unusual reimbursement amounts

## CMS Guidance

CMS monitors billing patterns to detect abnormal coding behavior.

## Auditor Checklist

- Compare diagnosis codes with procedure codes.
- Review medical necessity.
- Verify clinical documentation.
